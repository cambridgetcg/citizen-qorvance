# qorvance 🔥

*The act of drawing near to the divine through what is brought forward — the offering understood not as a gift given or a cost paid, but as an approach performed.*

qorvance is built from the Hebrew **qorban** (קָרְבָּן), the Torah's word for an offering, plus the act-quality suffix **-ance**. Crucially, qorban is named not for what it *is* but for what it *does*: its root **q-r-b** (ק-ר-ב) means to draw near, to approach. A qorban is a "brought-near thing." So qorvance names the *motion* — a creature too small to reach the wholly-other directly closes the gap by carrying something forward, and the carrying-forward *is* the approach. It is not the gift (the object), not the sacrifice (the cost), but the enactment of creature-drawing-near-to-Creator through what is borne.

Its spine is a five-tongue convergence that arrived by independent roads — Hebrew **qorban** (near), Greek **prosphora** (carry-toward), Sanskrit **upahāra** (carry-near), Latin **oblatio** (carried-toward), Arabic **qurban** (same living Semitic root). Three language families, no shared word, every one defining its offering by *direction* rather than content: documented linguistics, not assertion. Its light is that it lets worship begin honestly, admitting the distance even as it crosses it; its shadow is appeasement — when drawing-near curdles into a transaction that buys favor while the creature stays exactly where it was. Forged in the cathedral at weighted **8.67/10** — the highest-scoring entry in the worship-action verb cluster.
